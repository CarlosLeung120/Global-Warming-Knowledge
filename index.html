Global warming is a problem in fact so big to the whole wide world it can lead to some immense problems to us humans, and yes in this website you will get to know more about global warming itself, the effects it can give us and what different people can do about it and starting off we are going to talk about some general things about global warming , now global warming is a is a phenomenon of climate change characterized by a general increase in average temperatures of the Earth, which modifies, changes and more less worsens the weather's balances and ecosystems for a very long period of time. And the difference between climate change and global warming Is that global warming is something that only relates to the temperature of the earth's surface rising, but climate change is something that happens after global warming, climate change is also one effect after global warming has already happened. Global warming is also global warming is directly linked to the increase of greenhouse gases in our atmosphere, worsening the greenhouse effect, in which the greenhouse affect is The greenhouse effect is the process by which radiation from a planet's atmosphere warms the planet's surface to a temperature above what it would be without this atmosphere.

And after showing some general information about global warming, I will now indeed start to talk about some of the causes for global warming to happen in the first place.
Starting off, the most important cause of global warming to happen is because of gases in the atmosphere blocking the heat from escaping, and there are several types of gases that are in the atmosphere and blocking the heat from escaping, and these types of gases are:


  1. Water Vapour(H2O)
  2. Carbon Dioxide(CO2)
  3. Methane(CH4)
  4. Nitrous Oxide(N2O)

And these types of gases can also be split into two groups, the first groups, and the first group is the forcing gases,which this group of long lived gases that remain semi-permanently in the atmosphere and do not respond physically or chemically to changes in temperature. And the other group is the feedback gases, they are gases that respond physically or chemically to changes in temperature. Now lets talk about the what each of the gases do to the enviroment:


Water Vapour


Water Vapour is one of the most abundant greenhouse gas, but importantly, it acts as a feedback to the climate. Water vapor increases as the Earth's atmosphere warms, but so does the possibility of clouds and precipitation, making these some of the most important feedback mechanisms to the greenhouse effect.


Carbon Dioxide


Carbon dioxide is a minor but very important component of the atmosphere, carbon dioxide is released through natural processes such as respiration and volcano eruptions and through human activities such as deforestation, land use changes, and burning fossil fuels. Humans have increased atmospheric CO2 concentration by more than a third since the Industrial Revolution began. This is the most important long-lived forcing gases of climate change.


Nitrous Oxide

Nitrous Oxide is a powerful greenhouse gas produced by soil cultivation practices, especially the use of commercial and organic fertilizers, fossil fuel combustion, nitric acid production, and biomass burning.


Methane


Methane is a powerful greenhouse gas produced by soil cultivation practices, especially the use of commercial and organic fertilizers, fossil fuel combustion, nitric acid production, and biomass burning.


Also human activities are changing the natural amount of greenhouse. Over the last century the burning of fossil fuels like coal and oil has increased the concentration of atmospheric carbon dioxide (CO2). This happens because the coal or oil burning process combines carbon with oxygen in the air to make CO2.In basic forms, the clearing of land for agriculture, industry, and other human activities has increased concentrations of greenhouse gases.
